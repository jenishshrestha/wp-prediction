# France (Les Bleus) — Base Profile

_Last updated: 2026-06-16_

## Identity
- FIFA ranking: ~2–3 (June 2026)
- Elo rating: ~2040–2070 (top tier; among tournament favourites)
- Squad market value: ~€1.2bn+ (one of the two or three most valuable squads at the tournament)
- Manager: Didier Deschamps (final tournament of his tenure; 2018 world champion as coach)
- Tactical identity: 4-2-3-1 — double pivot for control, devastating speed and 1v1 quality in the front four; comfortable ceding possession to strike on transition.

## Unit Ratings (FIFA / EA FC style — OVR 85)
- **ATT 87** — Mbappé, Dembélé (2025 Ballon d'Or), Olise, Doué. Best attacking quartet in the field; multiple match-winners.
- **MID 84** — Tchouaméni + Rabiot screen; Doué/Olise drift inside. Strong on ball progression and duels; slightly light on a pure deep creator.
- **DEF 85** — Maignan (GK), Saliba + Upamecano (CB), Koundé + T. Hernández (FB). Elite pace and aerial security; arguably the best back line in the tournament.

## Qualification Path
Comfortable UEFA qualification as group winners; remained among the favourites throughout the cycle.

## Recent Major-Tournament History
- 2022 World Cup: runners-up (lost final to Argentina on penalties).
- Euro 2024: semi-final exit (low-scoring, criticised for over-reliance on individual moments).
- Pattern: defensively elite and ruthless on transition; occasionally low-scoring in open play despite the talent, leaning on Mbappé/Dembélé moments.

## Stable Tendencies
- High attacking ceiling driven by individual quality; scored multiple goals in 9 of their last 10.
- Very solid defensively; concede little to teams that come out to play.
- Can be passive/flat against deep blocks or in dead-rubber friendlies (see Ivory Coast loss).
