# Japan — Base Profile

_Last updated: 2026-06-13 (refreshed for WC2026 opener)_

## Identity
- FIFA ranking: ~17–19 (Asia's #1)
- Elo rating: ~1800 (strong by Asian standards; ~1620 reading from 2023 is now dated upward)
- Squad market value: ~€285m (avg ~€11m/player)
- Manager: Hajime Moriyasu (since 2021, contracted through 2026 cycle)
- Tactical identity: flexible 3-4-2-1 / 4-2-3-1, compact mid-block, fast vertical transitions, technical wide forwards

## Qualification Path
Qualified early from Asian qualifying as group winners (dominant: e.g. 6-0 Indonesia Jun 2025), one of the first teams to book a WC2026 place.

## Recent Major-Tournament History
- 2022 World Cup: beat Germany and Spain in group, lost R16 to Croatia on penalties.
- 2023 Asian Cup: QF exit.
- Recent form (2025-26) excellent: beat Brazil 3-2 (Oct 2025), Ghana 2-0, Bolivia 3-0; away wins at Scotland (1-0) and at England 1-0 at Wembley (Mar 2026); beat Iceland 1-0 (May 2026). Also lost 0-2 at USA (Sep 2025). Have scored in roughly 7 straight matches — a confident, in-form attack arriving as the form side of the two.

## Stable Tendencies
- Excellent in transition; dangerous on the counter against high lines.
- Defensively well-drilled in a low-mid block but can be physically out-muscled and vulnerable on crosses/set pieces against bigger sides.
- Goals tend to come in bursts via wide overloads; can go quiet vs disciplined elite defenses.
- Key injury notes (CONFIRMED 2026-06-13): **Wataru Endo is OUT injured** (correction to earlier "retirement" report — he is injured, not retired, but unavailable for the opener), removing the side's defensive-midfield screen and leadership. **Kaoru Mitoma OUT** (hamstring, suffered May 2026 vs Wolves) — a major attacking loss; Kubo expected to carry the creative load in his place. Takumi Minamino also reported unavailable. Tomiyasu has only just returned from ~2 years of injury, so depth is fit-but-rusty. Losing Endo + Mitoma (arguably their two best/most influential) materially blunts both the press-resistance in midfield and the left-sided attacking threat.
- Probable XI for the opener (3-4-2-1): Zion Suzuki; H. Ito, Watanabe, Taniguchi; Doan, Kamada, Tanaka, Nagatomo; Kubo, J. Ito; Ueda.
