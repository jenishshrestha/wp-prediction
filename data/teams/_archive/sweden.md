# Sweden — Base Profile

_Last updated: 2026-06-13_

## Identity
- FIFA ranking: ~38 (as of April 2026)
- Elo rating: ~1750–1780 (mid-tier European; approximate, not officially published)
- Squad market value: ~€350–400m (driven heavily by Isak + Gyökeres, two ~€70–100m strikers)
- Manager: Graham Potter (appointed October 2025)
- Tactical identity: Flexible under Potter — primary shape **3-4-1-2 / 3-4-2-1**, also uses 4-3-3 / 4-4-2. Possession-leaning but pragmatic; relies on two elite strikers and wing-back width to break low blocks.

## Qualification Path
Failed to qualify directly; rescued via the UEFA playoff path after Potter took over. Beat **Ukraine 3–1** (Gyökeres hat-trick) in the semi-final, then **Poland 3–2** in the final (Gyökeres 88' winner). Late, dramatic qualification on the back of attacking firepower.

## Recent Major-Tournament History
Missed WC2022 and Euro 2024; this is a return after a barren stretch. Historically a solid, organized tournament side (semi-finalists 1994, QF 2018) but the current squad is in transition — elite at striker, inexperienced and thin elsewhere.

## Stable Tendencies
- **Attack-heavy identity:** two world-class strikers make them dangerous against any defense; can score in bunches (3 goals in each playoff).
- **Defensive vulnerability:** conceded 1 and 2 in the playoffs, lost 1–3 to Norway and drew 2–2 with Greece in June friendlies — back line looks leaky and the system is still bedding in.
- Strong in transition and on crosses/set-pieces given target-man profile.
- June form is mixed/poor (L Norway, D Greece), so the team arrives without momentum despite the talent.
