# Qatar — Base Profile

_Last updated: 2026-06-13_

## Identity
- FIFA ranking: ~56 (June 2026)
- Elo rating: ~1550–1600 (well below the elite/top-20 tier)
- Squad market value: ~€25–35m (domestic-league based squad, very low by WC standards)
- Manager: Julen Lopetegui (Spanish; appointed for this cycle)
- Tactical identity: 4-3-3 / 5-3-2; possession-oriented at Asian level but reactive/low-block against stronger opposition. Reliant on Afif's creativity and Almoez Ali's finishing.

## Qualification Path
Qualified via the AFC route (Asian qualifying). Almoez Ali was the leading scorer in qualifying with 12 goals. Strong at the Asian level (won Asian Cup 2019 and 2023) but step up to World Cup tier is significant.

## Recent Major-Tournament History
- 2022 World Cup (hosts): lost all three group games (0-2 Ecuador, 1-3 Senegal, 0-2 Netherlands) — winless, eliminated bottom of group.
- Two-time AFC Asian Cup champions (2019, 2023) — dominant in Asia, untested vs European elite.
- Major step-up gap between continental and World Cup level remains.

## Stable Tendencies
- Comfortable with the ball vs Asian opposition but tends to sit deep and concede chances vs higher-ranked European/South American sides.
- Goal threat narrow: heavily dependent on Afif's supply and Almoez Ali's finishing.
- Defensively vulnerable to pace, crosses, and set pieces against top opposition.

## Live notes (as of 2026-06-13)
- Poor recent form: 1 win in last 5, no win since October 2025; 4 scored / 8 conceded in that run.
- Preparation heavily disrupted: cancelled/suspended friendlies (Argentina in March, Sudan in May) and protest-disrupted Ireland meeting amid regional conflict — limited match sharpness and cohesion.
