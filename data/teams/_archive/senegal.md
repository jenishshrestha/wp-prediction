# Senegal (Lions of Teranga) — Base Profile

_Last updated: 2026-06-16_

## Identity
- FIFA ranking: ~18–19 (June 2026; top African side alongside Morocco)
- Elo rating: ~1780–1820 (strong, below the elite tier)
- Squad market value: ~€350–400m
- Manager: Pape Thiaw
- Tactical identity: 4-3-3 — compact, physical, athletic. Functional midfield three, defends in a mid/low block and attacks with pace and direct running on the flanks.

## Unit Ratings (FIFA / EA FC style — OVR 79)
- **ATT 80** — Mané, Jackson, I. Sarr. Genuine pace and physicality, real counter threat; finishing can be wasteful against organised defences.
- **MID 77** — I. Gueye, P. Gueye, Pape Matar Sarr / Camara. Energetic, good in duels and transitions; less creative through the middle.
- **DEF 80** — Mendy (GK), Koulibaly + Niakhaté (CB), athletic fullbacks. Experienced, physical, hard to break down; Koulibaly aging but still a leader.

## Qualification Path
Qualified comfortably as one of Africa's strongest sides; deep, experienced squad blended with emerging youth (Mbaye, B. Ndiaye).

## Recent Major-Tournament History
- 2022 World Cup: Round of 16 (lost to England).
- AFCON 2021 winners; 2025 title initially claimed before a controversial CAF sanction.
- Pattern: defensively organised and physical, dangerous on the counter; can struggle to break down opponents and to finish chances cleanly.

## Stable Tendencies
- Strong defensive base and counter-attacking threat; awkward, physical opponent for any favourite.
- Goal output reliant on Mané/Sarr moments and Jackson's movement; finishing efficiency a recurring weakness.
- Capable of upsets (beat France 1–0 at the 2002 World Cup) but inconsistent against elite opposition.
