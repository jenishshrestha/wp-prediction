# Iraq (Lions of Mesopotamia) — Base Profile

_Last updated: 2026-06-16_

## Identity
- FIFA ranking: ~57–60 (June 2026)
- Elo rating: ~1560–1600
- Squad market value: ~€40–55m
- Manager: Graham Arnold (Australian; organised, pragmatic)
- Tactical identity: 4-2-3-1 — compact, disciplined low/mid block; defends in numbers and looks to counter through Hussein and Al-Hamadi. Hard-working and well-drilled rather than talent-rich.

## Unit Ratings (FIFA / EA FC style — OVR 72)
- **ATT 71** — Aymen Hussein (main goal threat), Ali Al-Hamadi (Luton), wide runners. Limited top-end quality; struggles to create and to finish against organised defences.
- **MID 72** — Zidane Iqbal (ex-Man Utd, creativity), Amir Al-Ammari. Tidy and industrious; Iqbal is the main spark but the unit is outmatched against elite midfields.
- **DEF 74** — Jalal Hassan (GK, 100+ caps) behind a deep, organised back line. The team's strongest, most cohesive unit — kept it tight against Spain (1–1).

## Qualification Path
Qualified as the **48th and final team**, beating Bolivia 2–1 in the inter-confederation play-off. First World Cup appearance since **1986 — a 40-year wait**.

## Recent Major-Tournament History
- No World Cup since 1986; best known continentally (2007 Asian Cup winners).
- Pattern: organised and competitive, but a major step up in class at World Cup level; low-scoring, defence-first identity under Arnold.

## Stable Tendencies
- Defensive discipline is the core strength; can frustrate stronger sides for long spells.
- Goal output is thin — relies on Hussein/Al-Hamadi moments and set pieces; can go scoreless against good defences.
- Vulnerable to lapses late in games (conceded twice in defeat to Venezuela).
