# Argentina (La Albiceleste) — Base Profile

_Last updated: 2026-06-16_

## Identity
- FIFA ranking: ~1 (June 2026; reigning world champions)
- Elo rating: ~2080–2110 (top of the tournament)
- Squad market value: ~€900m–1.0bn
- Manager: Lionel Scaloni
- Tactical identity: Flexible 4-3-3 / 4-4-2; for the opener Scaloni is reported to lean on a **back-three** setup (3-5-2 / 3-4-2-1) to add defensive cover. Controlled, midfield-dominant, lethal on the counter through Messi and Lautaro.

## Unit Ratings (FIFA / EA FC style — OVR 86)
- **ATT 87** — Messi (final World Cup, turns 39 mid-tournament), Lautaro Martínez, J. Álvarez, Almada, Simeone. Elite quality and depth; Messi a touch past his peak but still decisive.
- **MID 85** — De Paul, Enzo Fernández, Mac Allister. World-class, balanced, controls tempo; the engine of the 2022 title.
- **DEF 85** — Emi Martínez (GK), Romero, Otamendi, Lisandro Martínez, Molina, Tagliafico. Strong and experienced, but aging in the centre and carrying fitness doubts (see live notes).

## Qualification Path
Comfortable CONMEBOL campaign as one of the standout qualifiers. Defending the title won in Qatar 2022; kept **17 players** from that winning squad.

## Recent Major-Tournament History
- 2022 World Cup: **champions** (beat France on penalties).
- 2024 Copa América: champions.
- Pattern: tournament-hardened winners, elite midfield control, decisive in tight games; the open question is age/legs over a long tournament.

## Stable Tendencies
- Controls games through midfield and game-management; rarely beaten when ahead.
- Goals shared between Messi's creation and Lautaro's finishing; can score in bunches against weaker sides (5–0 Zambia).
- Defensive solidity is a hallmark, but the central pairing is aging and currently patched up.
