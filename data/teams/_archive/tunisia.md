# Tunisia — Base Profile

_Last updated: 2026-06-13_

## Identity
- FIFA ranking: ~44–45 (as of June 2026)
- Elo rating: ~1650–1680 (approximate; African mid-tier, not officially published)
- Squad market value: ~€100–130m (Europe-based core but no marquee €50m+ stars)
- Manager: Sabri Lamouchi (French-Tunisian; took charge January 2026)
- Tactical identity: **Defensive, compact 4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1**. Low-block, hard-working midfield, organized and difficult to break down. Threat mainly via transition, set-pieces, and full-back overlaps.

## Qualification Path
Won CAF Group H emphatically: **28 points from 30** (9 W, 1 D), 22 scored, and **zero goals conceded across all 10 matches** — the first team in World Cup history to qualify without conceding. Opponents were modest (Eq. Guinea, Namibia, Malawi, Liberia, São Tomé), so the clean-sheet feat is partly competition-quality, but the defensive organization is genuine.

## Recent Major-Tournament History
- **AFCON 2025:** eliminated in the last 16 on penalties; underwhelming (group: drew Tanzania 1–1, lost to Nigeria 3–2, drew Mali 1–1). Showed scoring/defensive frailty against better African sides.
- WC2022: group stage exit but beat France 1–0; WC2018 group exit. Established at being hard to beat but rarely advancing.

## Stable Tendencies
- **Defensively organized, low block** — the core identity; designed to keep games tight and steal a result.
- **Low scoring / blunt attack:** no elite finisher; goals come from midfield runners, set-pieces, and full-back Abdi. Struggle to break down or chase games.
- **Poor recent form vs quality:** lost 1–0 to Austria and **5–0 to Belgium** in June 2026 friendlies; drew 0–0 Canada, beat Haiti 1–0, drew 1–1 Brazil (Nov 2025). The Belgium thrashing exposed the back line against elite attackers.
