## Qualification Path
Qualified from European qualifying as group winner; comfortable campaign (e.g. Nov 2025: 1-1 Poland, 4-0 Lithuania). Strong attacking output against weaker sides.

## Recent Major-Tournament History
- Euro 2024: reached semi-finals.
- 2022 World Cup: quarter-finals (lost to Argentina on penalties).
- 2024-25 Nations League: lost QF/semi to Spain on penalties (3-3, 5-5 agg).
- Recent form underwhelming: Mar 2026 beat Norway 2-1, drew Ecuador 1-1; Jun 2026 final warm-ups lost 0-1 Algeria, then scraped past Uzbekistan 2-1 on two Gakpo penalties (incl. a last-minute spot-kick) — nerves visibly building entering the opener. Note the longer-range record: unbeaten in their last 16 WC group-stage matches (W12 D4) dating to 1994.

## Stable Tendencies
- Reliable scorers vs mid/lower opposition; strong from set pieces (Van Dijk).
- High defensive line can be exposed in transition by quick wide players.
- Notable absences entering WC2026 (CONFIRMED 2026-06-13): Xavi Simons (ruptured ACL, April), **Jurrien Timber (officially WITHDRAWN ~8 Jun, groin — Geertruida called up as replacement)**, Matthijs de Ligt (injury, out), Stefan de Vrij (injury, out), Schouten (ACL), Jeremie Frimpong (omitted by Koeman). The centre-back spine is gutted: Van Dijk (now fit) likely partners Van de Ven or Van Hecke — far less experienced cover than usual behind the customary high line. Depay's match fitness is the attacking question mark.
- Probable XI for the opener (4-2-3-1): Verbruggen; Dumfries, Van Hecke, Van Dijk, Van de Ven; De Jong, Gravenberch; Malen, Reijnders, Gakpo; Weghorst.
