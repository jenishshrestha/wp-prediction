## Identity
- FIFA ranking: ~29–32 (June 2026; rising fast)

- Squad market value: ~€500–550m

- Tactical identity: 4-3-3 — built to feed Haaland; Ødegaard orchestrates, pace and directness out wide (Nusa, Bobb). Strong going forward, more vulnerable defensively than their results suggest.

## Unit Ratings (FIFA / EA FC style — OVR 81)
- **ATT 84** — Haaland (55 intl goals; 16 in qualifying), Sørloth, Nusa, plus Ødegaard's creation. Elite top-end firepower; one of the best No.9s in the world leads the line.
- **MID 81** — Ødegaard (captain, playmaker), Berge, Thorstvedt. Quality and balance; Ødegaard is the creative hub.
- **DEF 78** — Nyland (GK), Ajer + Heggem (CB), Ryerson + Møller-Wolfe (FB). Competent but the clear weak link relative to the attack; can be exposed against quick, direct opponents.

## Qualification Path
Perfect UEFA campaign: **8 wins from 8**, 37 scored / 5 conceded (e.g. 5–0 Moldova, 4–2 Israel). First World Cup since 1998 — a 28-year wait ended by their golden generation.

## Recent Major-Tournament History
- No World Cup since 1998; missed recent Euros. This is a first major tournament for Haaland, Ødegaard and most of the core.
- Pattern: prolific in qualifying but untested on the biggest stage; tournament temperament is the open question.

## Stable Tendencies
- High goals-for ceiling driven by Haaland's finishing and Ødegaard's supply; score in bunches against teams that leave space.
- Defensively decent but not elite; the back line is the part most likely to be breached.
- Can be made to labour by deep, disciplined blocks that deny Haaland service.
