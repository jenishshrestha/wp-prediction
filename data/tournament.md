# FIFA World Cup 2026 — Tournament Reference

Reference data for predicting match scorelines. Compiled 2026-06-13 from FIFA and

## Format

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is the first edition contested by **48 teams** (up from 32).

- **Group stage:** 48 teams split into **12 groups of 4** (Groups A–L). Each team
  plays the other three in its group once (round-robin).
- **Advancement:** The **top 2 teams from each group** (24 teams) plus the **8 best
  third-placed teams** advance — a total of **32 teams**.
- **Knockout stage:** For the first time, the tournament includes a **Round of 32**,
  followed by Round of 16, quarter-finals, semi-finals, third-place play-off, and
  the final. All knockout matches are single-elimination (extra time, then penalties
  if level).
- Total matches: **104** (up from 64 in 2022).

## Hosts & Venues

Three host nations: **United States** (11 venues), **Mexico** (3), **Canada** (2) —
**16 stadiums** in 16 metro areas. The opener was at the Mexico City Stadium (Estadio
Azteca) on 11 June; the final is at New York/New Jersey Stadium (MetLife) on 19 July.

Stadiums carry generic "host" names for the tournament; the common name is shown in
parentheses. Matches run in peak North American summer, so heat/humidity and altitude
are material factors for several venues.

teams, alters passing/shooting). **Guadalajara** has a meaningful but lesser elevation
effect. Heat takeaway: Mexican venues plus southern/central US sites (Monterrey,
Dallas, Houston, Atlanta, Miami, Kansas City) can sap intensity in afternoon kickoffs;
several have roofs/A/C that reduce the effect.

### Confidence / uncertainty notes
- **High confidence:** Format (48/12/Round-of-32), host nations, stage dates, and the
  full Group A–L draw — all corroborated across FIFA, Al Jazeera, ESPN, Wikipedia.
- **Medium confidence:** Exact stadium altitude figures. Mexico City is widely cited at
  ~2,200–2,240 m; Guadalajara ~1,560 m and Monterrey ~540 m are general geographic
  elevations, not FIFA-published pitch figures. Treat as approximate.
- **Note:** Stadiums use generic tournament names due to FIFA sponsorship rules; the
  familiar name is given in parentheses for clarity.
