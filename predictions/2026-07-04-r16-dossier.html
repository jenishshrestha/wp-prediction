Reading the round

Where the edges are

  • Fatigue asymmetries are the round's alpha. Five of 16 teams played 120+ minutes in the R32 (MAR, PAR, BEL, ARG, EGY). Canada v Morocco and USA v Belgium are the ties where fresh legs face heavy ones.
  • Balogun's red is the only suspension that changes a first XI — the USA lose their 3-goal striker for a coin-flip tie.
  • Environment picks sides: Philadelphia's extreme heat helps Paraguay's block (slower siege); the Azteca's 2,240 m is Mexico's 12th man; roofs neutralise Houston, Arlington and Atlanta.
  • Defence-first survivors: Mexico (0 conceded), Spain (0), Colombia (1) — three of the four best defensive records meet high-rated attacks this round. Respect their modes: 1–0s and 1–1s, not routs.

Biggest uncertainties

  • Weather delays: MetLife (Sun) and the Azteca (Sun) both carry live thunderstorm risk; the Mexico–Ecuador tie was already halted an hour.
  • Late fitness calls: Davies (CAN, start/bench), Raphinha (BRA, game-time), Enciso (PAR), Nico Williams (ESP, 50/50), Salah's hamstring load (EGY).
  • Thin market: Switzerland–Colombia had no bookmaker three-way line at snapshot time — that W/D/W is the least anchored of the eight.
  • Pens branches: two headline draws (MEX–ENG, USA–BEL) are near-coin-flips on the shootout itself; treat the qualifier as ~55/45, not a conviction.
