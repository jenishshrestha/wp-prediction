Four angles worth knowing before you lock picks

1 · The all-defence coin-flips

  • Four ties are genuinely level low-event games headlined as draws: Mexico–Ecuador, Australia–Egypt, plus Ivory Coast–Norway and Portugal–Croatia. We respect the draw only where the W/D/W is truly balanced — and all four project toward extra time / penalties.
  • The two lowest-scoring projections — Mexico–Ecuador and Colombia–Ghana — pit two block-first sides against each other; both lean Under 2.5.

2 · Injuries that move the lines

  • Salah (Egypt) is the single biggest swing — out turns Australia–Egypt into a coin flip; fit, it shades Egypt.
  • Mendy's keeper doubt lifts Belgium; Ødegaard's sharpness gates Ivory Coast–Norway; England's right-back (James + Livramento out) is the one seam DR Congo can attack.

3 · Don't over-credit inflated attacks

  • Germany's 10, Canada's 8, Belgium's 6, Spain's 5, Portugal's 6 and Argentina's 8 are partly minnow-inflated. Against the stubborn blocks they now face — Paraguay, South Africa, Senegal, Cape Verde, Ghana — margins compress.
  • xG priors here are built off each opponent's actual group GA, not raw SoFIFA, which is why several favourites are headlined to win by a single goal.

4 · The marquee toss-ups

  • Netherlands–Morocco — Hakimi's overlaps against a Dutch left weakened by Timber's absence; the closest tie of the round.
  • Portugal–Croatia — two vintage midfields, both low-event; a single moment (set-piece, Modrić, Bruno) likely decides a game that wants to go the distance.
