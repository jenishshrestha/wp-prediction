The honest caveat

Be honest about the concentration: all four picks lean Argentina (Messi in two). These aren't four independent calls — they're essentially one bet (Argentina go all the way) plus its knock-ons, so if Argentina fall short, expect them to miss together. That's the bracket talking: the one contender on the softest path in the less-crowded half tends to see its stars sweep the gold — exactly what Argentina did in 2022 (Messi the Golden Ball, Emi Martínez the Glove). The built-in hedge is the Golden Boot, which most often escapes the champion — it went to Mbappé in 2022 even as Argentina won, which is why it's flagged a toss-up here. And a champion call from the Round of 32 is high-variance: the France–Germany and Spain–Portugal ties in the loaded left half are the hinges the whole thing swings on — if a France or Spain survives that, Mbappé or Yamal inherit the individual gold.
