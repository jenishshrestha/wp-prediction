The honest caveat

Three of the four picks are one bet — "France go all the way" — plus its knock-ons (Mbappé's Ball and Maignan's Glove both die with a France exit), hedged by Messi's Boot, which survives most brackets because his lead is already banked. The whole slip swings on two hinges: the probable France–Spain semi-final — Spain haven't conceded a goal in five matches, and if they survive it, Yamal/Simón inherit the individual gold — and the final itself, where the projected opponent is Argentina: a 2022 rematch in which the holders' path (Egypt, then Switzerland/Colombia) is soft enough that they arrive near-certain. If Argentina win that rematch instead, they sweep exactly as the pre-R32 version of this page predicted: title, Messi's Ball and Boot. Nothing here is 100% — a champion call four rounds out tops out near 30%, and these probabilities are judgment estimates, not model output.
